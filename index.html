Abstract

We propose Visulogic, a new benchmark for evaluating the visual reasoning capabilities of Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs). Visulogic contains 1,000 questions, which are divided into 6 main categories and 24 subcategories, allowing for a detailed evaluation of the models' performance. In comparison to previous benchmarks for MLLMs, our benchmark is more difficult to articulate through language and places a stronger emphasis on visual logical reasoning. We collect and curate Visulogic from the Internet under the premise of adhering to relevant protocols and agreements. The performance of most open-source or closed-source MLLMs is below 30%, whereas the human accuracy rate stands at 51.4% for comparison. This gap highlights the fact that MLLMs still exhibit significant limitations in their visual reasoning capabilities. Furthermore, we conduct various comparative experiments and detailed experimental analyses in this paper.
